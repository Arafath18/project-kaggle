Biography of Alan Turing

Alan Turing was a pioneering British mathematician, logician, and computer scientist whose work shaped the modern digital age.

Key Figures:
- Alan Turing: The subject, main mathematician and cryptanalyst.
- Joan Clarke: Fellow cryptanalyst and close friend at Bletchley Park.
- Winston Churchill: Prime Minister who provided resources to the cryptanalyst team.
- Max Newman: Mathematician who led the development of Colossus.

Timeline of Important Dates:
- June 23, 1912: Alan Turing is born in Maida Vale, London.
- September 4, 1939: Turing reports to Bletchley Park to begin codebreaking work.
- 1941: Turing and his team successfully crack the German naval Enigma code.
- 1945: Awarded an OBE by King George VI for his wartime services.
- 1950: Publishes his landmark paper "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," introducing the Turing Test.
- June 7, 1954: Turing dies in Wilmslow, England, at the age of 41.
